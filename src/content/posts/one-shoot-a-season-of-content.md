When a brand asks for "a video," what they usually need is a *season*. A single
hero film is a beautiful thing, but it lands once. The teams I work with have
channels to feed, a launch to support, and a sponsor deck to fill — and they
need all of it from one trip to the mountain.

So I plan the shoot as an ecosystem from the first conversation, not as a single
deliverable I'll slice up later.

## What one production can become

- **A hero film** — the three-to-five-minute centrepiece for the launch page,
  festival, or sponsor pitch.
- **Vertical cutdowns** — captioned, built for how people actually watch on a
  phone, not letterboxed afterthoughts.
- **Social clips** — short, specific moments that work on their own.
- **Graded stills** — for press, e-commerce, and partnership decks.
- **Channel- or sponsor-specific edits** — the same story, cut for the audience
  that's paying attention.

## Why it's planned, not salvaged

You can't fake verticals out of a film shot only for 16:9, and you can't grade
stills out of footage shot without them in mind. Capturing for the whole
ecosystem means thinking about every ratio and every frame on the mountain —
while the light and the effort are real — instead of trying to rescue formats in
the edit.

The result is the same budget producing a campaign's worth of assets, all
carrying one consistent voice. That's the difference between buying a video and
commissioning a body of work.

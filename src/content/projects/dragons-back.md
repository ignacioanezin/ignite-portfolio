challenge: "Sierra Skyrace had a brutal, beautiful course and almost no reach beyond a few hundred local runners. The organizers needed international entrants and sponsor interest, but their previous recap videos were drone-heavy highlight reels that looked like every other race on the calendar. Nothing made you feel the mountain."
strategy: "We threw out the highlight-reel format. Instead of covering the race from the outside, I ran a chest-and-helmet rig through the queen stage and built the film around a single mid-pack runner's effort — the doubt at the col, the recovery on the descent. The race becomes a character. The landscape stops being a backdrop and becomes the antagonist."
production: "I ran the course. That's the part an external crew can't fake — the camera is inside the effort, breathing where the athletes breathe, on the exposed ridgeline you can't reach with a vehicle or a long lens. Two days of recon on foot to scout light, then a single race-day capture: body-mounted A-cam through the technical sections, a small second unit on the two accessible cols, FPV drone only where it earned its place."
deliverables:
  - "5-minute hero film (festival + sponsor decks)"
  - "Three 45-second social cutdowns (vertical, captioned)"
  - "15 graded stills for press + start-list marketing"
  - "Sponsor-specific edit isolating brand moments"
result: "Next edition's international entries grew sharply and two outdoor brands came on as sponsors after seeing the film in the organizer's deck. The hero film became the race's primary marketing asset for the entire season."

The mountain doesn't care that you brought a camera. That was the whole idea —
to make a film that respected the effort instead of flattening it into a montage.

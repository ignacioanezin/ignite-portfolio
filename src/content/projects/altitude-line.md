excerpt: "A product launch that didn't look like an ad — and outperformed the brand's paid campaign on its own."
challenge: "Cumbre was launching a high-altitude shell and wanted launch content. Their instinct was a studio product film: clean, lit, spinning garment on a turntable. The problem is nobody shares a turntable. They needed people to feel why the jacket exists, in the conditions it was built for, without it reading as a commercial."
strategy: "We sold the conditions, not the product. The film follows two athletes on a high approach as the weather turns — the shell shows up because it's needed, never because the script demands a hero shot. Product as a consequence of the story. The brand logo appears for a total of four seconds across a three-minute film, and that restraint is exactly why it converted."
production: "Shot over three days at altitude, carrying everything in. Being a runner myself meant I could move fast and light with the athletes at 4,000m instead of slowing the shoot to a crawl — we caught the real weather window instead of staging it. Natural light only, handheld, close. The cold is real in the frame because it was real on the mountain."
deliverables:
  - "3-minute brand film (hero placement, launch page + YouTube)"
  - "Six product-detail social clips (the shell in genuine use)"
  - "Lookbook stills for e-commerce and wholesale"
  - "16:9 and 9:16 masters for paid + organic"
result: "The organic hero film drove more launch-week engagement than the brand's paid creative, and the shell sold through its first production run ahead of forecast. Cumbre moved their following launch to the same embedded approach."

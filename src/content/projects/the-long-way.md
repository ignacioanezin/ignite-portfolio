challenge: "A regional ultrarunner coming back from a year out with injury had a story worth telling and no way to tell it. She needed a piece that introduced her to brands as more than a results sheet — something that communicated who she is, not just how fast she runs. The honest stuff is hard to film without it turning into a sob story."
strategy: "We built a quiet, first-person portrait around a single long run in Patagonia. No talking-head crew, no staged tears — just her voice over the effort and the landscape doing the emotional work. The injury is context, not the subject. The subject is what makes someone go back out there anyway. Restraint kept it honest."
production: "I ran most of the route alongside her, which is the only way to get a portrait this close without a crew breaking the spell. When it's just the two of us moving through the valley, she forgets the camera is there — and that's where the real film is. Early light, long lenses for the landscape, intimate handheld for the running."
deliverables:
  - "6-minute athlete portrait (pitch + festival)"
  - "Sponsor-pitch cutdown (90 seconds)"
  - "Vertical social series introducing her to a wider audience"
  - "Portrait stills for press and partnership decks"
result: "She signed her first apparel sponsorship within months of releasing the film, with the brand citing the portrait directly. The piece now anchors her partnership pitches."
